**Diagnosis met (§ 2.1)?**
The diagnosis requirement is satisfied by demonstrating cervical
radiculopathy, cervical myelopathy, or disc herniation with neurological
deficit.

Walking through each option:
- Cervical radiculopathy: requires radicular symptoms in dermatomal
  distribution with nerve root compression on imaging. The case has no
  dermatomal sensory loss documented, no motor deficit, no positive
  Spurling's test, and no nerve root impingement on MRI. Not met.
- Cervical myelopathy: requires signs of cord involvement. The case has
  no Hoffmann sign, no clonus, no gait disturbance, no bowel/bladder
  involvement, and no myelomalacia signal on MRI. Not met.
- Disc herniation with neurological deficit: requires structural disc
  herniation correlated with deficit. The MRI shows small disc bulge
  without herniation, and no neurological deficit is documented. Not met.

Diagnosis requirement is NOT met.
